### STAY SAFE ONLINE – PREVENTION CHECKLIST



\--------------------------------------------------



### GENERAL SAFETY TIPS:



• Never share OTP or PIN with anyone  

OTP and PIN are confidential details. No bank or official service will ever ask for them. Sharing them can lead to immediate loss of money.



• Do not click on unknown links  

Fraudsters often send fake links through messages or emails. Clicking on such links can lead to hacking or data theft.



• Always verify payment requests  

Before accepting any payment request, check the sender’s details carefully. Do not approve requests from unknown people.



• Use only official apps and websites  

Always download apps from trusted sources like Play Store or App Store. Avoid using unknown or third-party applications.



• Enable two-factor authentication  

This adds an extra layer of security to your accounts and makes it harder for hackers to access them.



• Regularly check bank statements  

Keep track of your transactions to quickly identify any suspicious activity.



• Avoid public Wi-Fi for transactions  

Public networks are not secure and can be used by hackers to steal your data.



• Keep your apps and devices updated  

Updates fix security issues and protect your device from new threats.



\--------------------------------------------------



### FINANCIAL / UPI SAFETY TIPS:



• Never approve “collect requests” without checking  

Scammers often send fake requests to receive money. Always read carefully before approving any request.



• Do not trust unknown calls asking for payments  

If someone asks for money or bank details over a call, do not trust them without proper verification.



\--------------------------------------------------



### REPORT CYBERCRIME:



• Website: https://cybercrime.gov.in  

You can report any online fraud or cybercrime on this official government website.



• Helpline Number: 1930  

Call this number immediately if you become a victim of financial fraud.



\--------------------------------------------------



### Conclusion:



Being aware and alert is the best way to stay safe online. By following these simple steps, students can protect their personal information and avoid becoming victims of cybercrime.
