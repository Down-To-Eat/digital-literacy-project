## **UPI FRAUD**



UPI fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India today, mainly because digital payments have become very common and easy to use. Unified Payments Interface (UPI) allows people to instantly send and receive money using mobile apps, which makes life convenient—but it also creates opportunities for fraudsters. UPI fraud happens when criminals trick people into sending money or sharing sensitive details like OTPs, PINs, or approving payment requests without realizing they are being scammed.



Most UPI frauds rely on a technique called Social Engineering. This means the scammer does not hack the system directly but instead manipulates the victim emotionally or psychologically. For example, a fraudster may call pretending to be a friend, bank official, delivery agent, or even a government officer. They speak politely and confidently to gain trust. Sometimes, they create a sense of urgency by saying something like “Your account will be blocked” or “You need to act immediately to receive money.” Because of this pressure, people often act quickly without thinking carefully.



One common method used in UPI fraud is sending a “collect request” instead of a payment. The victim believes they are receiving money, but in reality, they are approving a request to send money. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money gets transferred to the scammer’s account. In other cases, fraudsters ask for OTPs or ask the victim to install screen-sharing apps, giving them access to the victim’s phone and banking apps. All these methods rely on confusion, trust, and lack of awareness.



UPI fraud can affect anyone, but certain groups are more vulnerable. Elderly people, students, and those who are not very familiar with digital technology are often targeted. However, even educated and tech-savvy individuals can fall victim if they are not alert. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, mental stress, and loss of confidence in using online payment systems.



To stay safe from UPI fraud, it is important to follow some basic precautions :



1. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details with anyone, no matter how trustworthy they seem. 
2. Always double-check before approving any payment request. 
3. Remember that to receive money, you do not need to enter your PIN—PIN is only required when sending money.
4. Avoid answering unknown calls related to banking or payments, and never click on suspicious links.
5. If someone pressures you to act quickly, take a moment to think and verify their identity.



In conclusion, UPI is a powerful and convenient tool, but it must be used carefully. Awareness is the best protection against fraud. By understanding how scams work and staying alert, people can enjoy the benefits of digital payments without falling victim to cybercriminals.



## **Real Life Incident**



The Bhopal UPI fraud case clearly shows how cybercriminals don’t always need advanced technology to cheat people—they often rely on simple tricks and human emotions. In this incident, which happened in Bhopal, a retired man lost around ₹1.45 lakh to a fraudster who pretended to be someone he knew. The scammer called him from an unknown number but spoke in a very friendly and familiar manner. Because of this, the victim felt comfortable and trusted the caller. This situation highlights how easily people can be misled when trust and emotions come into play.



The fraud was carried out using a technique called social engineering. Instead of hacking into any system, the scammer manipulated the victim’s mind. He spoke politely, confidently, and in a convincing tone, slowly building trust during the conversation. Step by step, he guided the victim to perform certain actions on his phone. Since the victim believed he was talking to a known person, he didn’t find anything suspicious. This shows that many scammers depend more on understanding human behavior than on technical skills.



During the call, the fraudster instructed the victim to carry out certain steps related to UPI transactions. The victim was probably asked to approve payment requests or enter his UPI PIN, thinking it was part of a genuine process. However, in reality, he was unknowingly allowing money to be transferred to the scammer’s account. Within a very short time, ₹1.45 lakh was deducted from his bank account, and he didn’t even realize what had happened until it was too late.



Another major factor in this case was the use of urgency and pressure. The scammer made sure that the victim acted quickly, without giving him enough time to think or double-check anything. This is a very common trick used in fraud cases. When people feel rushed or pressured, they often make quick decisions and ignore warning signs. In this case, the victim trusted the caller and followed instructions without questioning them, which led to the loss.



This incident also proves that such scams are not limited to uneducated people. The victim was a retired person who likely had life experience and basic financial knowledge. Still, he became a target of this fraud. This shows that anyone can fall victim, especially when scammers use emotional manipulation and smart communication. It also points out that many people still lack proper awareness about how digital payment systems like UPI actually work.



There are some very important lessons we can learn from this case. First, never trust unknown calls, even if the person sounds familiar or claims to know you. Always verify their identity before doing anything. Second, remember that no genuine person, bank, or organization will ever ask you to approve payment requests or share sensitive details like OTPs or your UPI PIN. Third, never act in a hurry. Always take a moment to think and confirm before making any financial decision.



In conclusion, the Bhopal UPI fraud case teaches us that being alert and aware is the best way to stay safe in today’s digital world. People should always question anything unusual, understand how online payments work, and avoid making decisions under pressure. By staying careful and informed, we can protect ourselves from such scams and keep our money safe from cybercriminals.
